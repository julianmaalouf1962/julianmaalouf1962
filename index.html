Pattern Language  ·  Christopher Alexander  ·  Design Patterns

The book that accidentally shaped the internet

In 1977, an architect named Christopher Alexander published A Pattern Language — a catalogue of 253 recurring solutions to problems in building design. Twenty years later, software engineers cited it as the inspiration for one of programming's most influential ideas. Neither group fully understood what the other was doing. That gap is worth examining.
